Community

Where Remote Developers Hang Out Online

June 20, 2026 · 5 min read

Discords, newsletters, and forums worth a remote developer’s attention in 2026—and which communities to skip.

Community is how remote developers hear about roles early, sanity-check offers, and stay sharp. Focus on a few high-signal places instead of every Discord invite.

Worth your time

  • Specialty Discords / Slacks for your stack (e.g. framework or cloud communities) with active job channels
  • Newsletters that list remote roles with salary when available
  • GitHub Discussions / forums tied to tools you use daily
  • Local-to-global meetups that record talks for async viewing

Usually skip

  • Huge “remote jobs” Discords that are 90% spam
  • Communities that ban salary talk
  • Engagement-farm LinkedIn carousels with no substance

How to participate

Answer questions in public, share what you ship, and keep a short “I’m open to roles” blurb ready. Recruiters notice consistent helpers more than cold applicants.
