The Operator position is a position for new hires with significant related experience or significant technical education in related fields. The Operator shall have the ability to gain a thorough understanding of the process, including knowledge of the mechanical and electrical systems at the plant. The Plant Operator is responsible for carrying out basic Operations and maintenance tasks throughout the facility. The Operator position includes but not limited to the monitoring and oversight of the plants, starting and stopping equipment, issue troubleshooting and resolution, and decision making on minor process changes.

Requirements

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:

  • Take shifts of full responsibility for monitoring the plant operations as necessary and directed.
  • Start and Stop equipment as necessary and as directed.
  • Make adjustments to the process as necessary and/or directed.
  • Use daily checklists to appropriately and accurately record plant data flows, pressures and other measurements of the process and equipment. Responsible for reporting data as required by the company.
  • Make daily observations of the equipment and process to ensure that it is working correctly and safely. This includes but is not limited to looking for leaking pipes, fittings and equipment, unsafe conditions, non-standard data, unusual vibrations, sounds, smells or operating temperatures.
  • Report any improper working equipment or unsafe situations as described above to the supervisor or other designated people as appropriate and document such in IFS or KPA
  • Replace consumable products such as filter elements and chemicals.
  • Take company provided training courses in environmental health and safety and comply with safe working practices.
  • Work on various projects to keep the plant neat, clean and safe. These include but are not limited to removing corrosion, brushing, sweeping, sanding, painting, tightening, landscaping and other types of manual and semi-skilled labor.
  • Engage in preventive maintenance as directed.
  • Engage in corrective maintenance and repair as directed; inclusive of, but not limited to, mechanical or electrical.
  • Engage in equipment service and repair as directed.
  • Keep track of inventory by marking down or reporting to the Plant Manager any items used or items that the company is low on in order to ensure that adequate spare parts, consumables lubricants etc. are kept on site in adequate supply.
  • Be positive, pleasant to co-workers and customer service oriented and to the extent that there is normal contact with the customer. Report any customer feedback, good or bad.
